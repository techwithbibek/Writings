## Advanced English Words

1. **Meticulous** – very careful and precise

* She is meticulous about checking her work.

2. **Inevitable** – certain to happen

* Failure is inevitable if you don’t prepare.

3. **Ambiguous** – unclear or having more than one meaning

* His answer was ambiguous.

4. **Plausible** – believable; possible

* Your explanation sounds plausible.

5. **Intrinsic** – essential or natural part of something

* Creativity is intrinsic to good design.

6. **Alleviate** – reduce or ease (pain, problems)

* This medicine will alleviate your headache.

7. **Indispensable** – absolutely necessary

* English is indispensable for many jobs.

8. **Impeccable** – perfect, without mistakes

* Her pronunciation is impeccable.

9. **Condescending** – talking as if others are less intelligent

* Don’t be condescending toward your coworkers.

10. **Resilient** – able to recover quickly

* Children are surprisingly resilient.

11. **Feasible** – possible or workable

* Your plan is not feasible.

12. **Scrutinize** – examine closely

* The teacher scrutinized the essays.

13. **Reluctant** – unwilling or hesitant

* He was reluctant to try something new.

14. **Candid** – honest and direct

* Thank you for your candid feedback.

15. **Improvise** – create without preparation

* The actors had to improvise the scene.

16. **Repercussion** – negative result of an action

* Your decision may have serious repercussions.

17. **Deteriorate** – become worse

* The weather is deteriorating.

18. **Formidable** – powerful and impressive

* She is a formidable opponent.

19. **Proficient** – skilled or good at something

* She is proficient in three languages.

20. **Arduous** – very difficult and tiring

* Climbing the mountain was an arduous task.

21. **Pragmatic** – practical; based on realistic solutions

* We need a pragmatic approach.

22. **Astute** – very smart, quick to understand

* He made an astute observation.

23. **Perceive** – become aware; understand

* How do you perceive this situation?

24. **Conspicuous** – noticeable, easily seen

* Her bright dress made her conspicuous.

25. **Articulate** – able to express ideas clearly

* He is very articulate when speaking.

  
26. **Ambiguous**

Meaning: unclear; having more than one possible meaning
* His message was ambiguous, so no one knew what he meant.

27. **Astounding**

Meaning: very surprising; amazing
* Example: The magician’s performance was absolutely astounding.

28. **Bewildering**

Meaning: very confusing; hard to understand
* Example: The city’s subway system is bewildering for visitors.

29. **Elaborate**

Meaning: very detailed; carefully planned
* Example: They created an elaborate plan for the event.

30. **Exceptional**

Meaning: outstanding; unusually good
* Example: She is an exceptional student.

31. **Explicit**

Meaning: very clear and direct; leaving no doubt
* Example: The teacher gave explicit instructions.

32. **Exquisite**

Meaning: extremely beautiful or delicate
*Example: The artist’s painting is exquisite.

33. **Flawless**

Meaning: perfect; without any mistakes
* Example: Her performance was flawless.

34. **Formidable**

Meaning: strong, powerful, or impressive in a way that causes fear or respect
* Example: He is a formidable opponent in chess.

35. **Gripping**

Meaning: very exciting or interesting; holding your attention
* Example: The movie was so gripping that I couldn’t look away.

36. **Heartwarming**

Meaning: causing feelings of happiness and emotional warmth
* Example: Their reunion after many years was heartwarming.

37. **Immense**

Meaning: extremely large; huge
* Example: The stadium can hold an immense crowd.

38. **Indispensable**

Meaning: absolutely necessary; essential
* Example: A good dictionary is indispensable for learning new words.

39. **Intricate**

Meaning: very detailed and complicated
* Example: The watch has an intricate design.

40. **Lucid**

Meaning: clear and easy to understand
* Example: Her explanation was so lucid that everyone understood.
